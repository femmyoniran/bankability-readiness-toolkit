About This Toolkit

Purpose

The Bankability Assessment Toolkit is designed to help energy infrastructure developers, utilities, and financial advisors evaluate the financing readiness of grid-scale energy projects. It addresses the persistent capital gap facing rural and underserved utilities by providing structured analysis tools aligned with federal financing programs.

Assessment Methodology

The bankability score is a weighted composite of five assessment dimensions, each scored on a 0-100 scale:

Dimension Weight Key Factors
Technology & Performance 20% Technology readiness, capacity factor, availability, permitting status
Financial Strength 25% DSCR, IRR, NPV, leverage ratio, debt yield, payback period
Credit & Counterparty 20% Offtaker credit rating, contract tenor, revenue concentration, regulatory stability
Project Structure 20% EPC contract, guarantees, insurance, reserves, lender protections
Market & Resource 15% Resource quality, interconnection certainty, curtailment risk, grid congestion

Financing Programs

USDA RUS Electric Program

The Rural Utilities Service provides direct loans and loan guarantees to rural electric cooperatives, public utility districts, and tribal utilities. The toolkit generates data templates compatible with Form 201 loan application requirements under 7 CFR Part 1710.

DOE LPO Title XVII

The Loan Programs Office provides loan guarantees for projects that employ innovative energy technologies under Title XVII of the Energy Policy Act of 2005. The toolkit generates pre-application and full application data structures aligned with 10 CFR Part 609 requirements.

Limitations

This toolkit provides analytical outputs for planning and evaluation purposes. Assessment results do not constitute financial advice, credit opinions, or lending commitments. Users should engage qualified financial, legal, and engineering advisors before making financing decisions. Federal program eligibility is determined solely by the respective agencies upon formal application review.
